African
Regions

Conventionally, there are five main geographical
regions or subregions in Africa. There are forty-eight
(48) countries that share the area of mainland Africa,
plus six (6) island nations are considered to be part of
the continent. All in all, there are fifty-four (54)
sovereign African countries and two (2) disputed areas,
namely Somaliland (autonomous region of Somalia) and Western
Sahara (occupied by Morocco and claimed by the Polisario).
